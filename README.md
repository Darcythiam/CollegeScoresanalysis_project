# Descriptive Analysis of CollegeScores4yr Dataset

## Overview

This project explores the **CollegeScores4yr** dataset from the Lock5Data collection using descriptive statistics and visualizations. The goal was to answer 10 practical questions about college cost, graduation rates, admission patterns, student debt, enrollment, and federal aid.

The analysis focuses on making higher education data easier to understand through summary statistics, plots, and correlations.

---

## Dataset

The dataset used in this project is the **CollegeScores4yr** dataset from Lock5Data.

It includes variables related to:

- Tuition
- Graduation rates
- Net price
- Admission rates
- Student debt
- Enrollment
- Pell Grant participation
- Public vs private institution type

Source used in the notebook:
`https://www.lock5stat.com/datasets3e/CollegeScores4yr.csv`

---

## Questions Answered

This project answers the following 10 questions:

1. Do private colleges really charge more than public colleges on average?
2. How many schools have graduation rates below 50%?
3. What is the standard deviation of average net price?
4. How uneven is the admission rate distribution?
5. What is the variance of admission rates?
6. Does higher tuition lead to higher graduation rates?
7. What is the proportion of private vs public colleges?
8. What is the average percentage of students receiving Pell Grants?
9. What is the distribution of student enrollment sizes?
10. Do colleges with higher graduation rates also have less debt?

## Key Takeaways

Some of the main patterns explored in this project include:

- Private colleges tend to charge more than public colleges
- A noticeable number of schools have graduation rates below 50%
- Admission rates vary widely across institutions
- Tuition and graduation rate show a small positive relationship
- Colleges with stronger graduation outcomes may also show slightly lower student debt
- Pell Grant usage highlights how many students rely on financial aid
